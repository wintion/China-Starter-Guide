# A‑Class Guide Rewrite Standard (A+ One‑Pass)

This document defines the **single, authoritative prompt and quality standard** for rewriting or generating **A‑class operational guides** on ChinaStarterGuide.

A‑class guides include:

* Payment
* Transport
* Life / Tools

These guides are **not articles**. They are **real‑world operation manuals** for first‑time foreigners.

---

## 1. Purpose

The goal of this standard is to ensure that all A‑class guides:

* Are **trustworthy in real use**, not just readable
* Minimize user trial‑and‑error
* Match or exceed the quality of existing anchor guides
* Can be generated by Codex **in a single pass**

---

## 2. Quality Anchors (Mandatory Alignment)

Every A‑class guide MUST match or exceed the operational detail and trust level of these anchors:

1. `how-to-pay-in-china.mdx`
2. `metro-basics.mdx`
3. `best-apps-for-foreigners.mdx`

If a guide is lower quality than any of these, it is considered **FAILED**.

---

## 3. A‑Class Mindset

An A‑class guide is written with these assumptions:

* The reader is **physically in China or about to arrive**
* The reader will **follow instructions exactly**
* Any missing step or ambiguity may cause real‑world failure
* Background explanations are less valuable than actionable detail

Do **not** write as a blogger, travel writer, or explainer.
Write as someone who has already failed and is preventing others from failing.

---

## 4. Mandatory Structure (All Required)

Every A‑class guide MUST include all of the following sections:

1. **Quick Answer**

   * 2–3 blunt sentences
   * No background, no justification

2. **Exact Actions**

   * Step‑by‑step
   * UI‑level (exact buttons, menus, labels)
   * Screen‑aware (what the user sees)

3. **Failure Scenarios & Fixes**

   * Multiple realistic failures
   * Each includes: reason → next action

4. **Reality Check**

   * Real situations that surprise foreigners
   * Each point ends with an immediate decision

5. **What Locals Do Instead**

   * Conditional behavior
   * Written as: *If X happens, locals do Y because Z*

6. **Checklist**

   * Only actionable, checkable items
   * No abstract states or advice

7. **Next Steps**

   * At least 5 internal links
   * Action‑oriented, not “related reading”

Missing any section = **FAILED**.

---

## 5. Operational Detail Requirements

### 5.1 UI‑Level Precision (Non‑Negotiable)

A‑class guides MUST include:

* Exact button names
* Menu paths
* Labels the user sees on screen
* Visual cues (icons, colors, timers, messages)

Unacceptable examples:

* “Set up the app”
* “Enable payment”

Acceptable examples:

* “Tap **Me** → **Bank Cards** → **Add International Card**”
* “A black‑and‑white QR code appears with a countdown timer”

---

### 5.2 Failure‑Driven Writing

Every major action MUST answer:

* What can fail?
* Why does it fail?
* What should the user do next?

If a step has no failure path, the guide is incomplete.

---

### 5.3 Decision Moments

A‑class guides explicitly cover **decision points**, such as:

* When to switch apps
* When to stop retrying
* When to change transport/payment strategy

Readers should never hesitate or guess.

---

## 6. Language Red Lines

The following indicate low‑trust content and are NOT allowed:

* “widely accepted”
* “very convenient”
* “generally speaking”
* “it is recommended to…”

Replace opinions with actions and consequences.

---

## 7. A+ One‑Pass Rewrite Prompt (Use This)

This is the **only prompt** that should be used to rewrite or generate A‑class guides.

```
Context:
- Read SPEC.md and CODEX.md first.
- This is a CRITICAL A-class operational guide for first-time foreigners.

Reference quality anchors (must match or exceed their level of trust and detail):
1) src/content/guides/payment/how-to-pay-in-china.mdx
2) src/content/guides/transport/metro-basics.mdx
3) src/content/guides/life/best-apps-for-foreigners.mdx

Target file:
<path-to-target-guide>

Task:
Rewrite the target file FROM SCRATCH as an A+ class operational manual.
This must fully replace the existing content.

Mandatory quality requirements:
1) Operational mindset: write as a real-world manual, not an article.
2) UI-level precision: exact buttons, menus, labels, and screen cues.
3) Failure-driven writing: every major action includes failure and fix.
4) Decision moments:
   - Reality Check: real situations with immediate actions.
   - What Locals Do Instead: conditional behavior (WHEN / WHY / WHAT).

Required structure (all sections mandatory):
- Quick Answer
- Exact Actions
- Failure Scenarios & Fixes
- Reality Check
- What Locals Do Instead
- Checklist
- Next Steps (5+ internal links)

Hard constraints:
- Remove all generic statements.
- Do not add background explanations.
- Do not modify routing, components, or other files.
- Keep frontmatter schema valid.

Acceptance criteria:
- The reader can complete the task without guessing.
- The guide is not lower quality than the three anchor guides.
- `npm run build` must still pass.
```

---

## 8. Review Rule

If a guide fails any of the following, it must be rewritten:

* Cannot be followed step‑by‑step in real life
* Lacks clear failure recovery
* Reads like a blog or overview
* Feels interchangeable with content for another country

---

## 9. Status

This document is the **final authority** for A‑class guide quality on ChinaStarterGuide.

No A‑class guide should be committed unless it complies with this standard.
