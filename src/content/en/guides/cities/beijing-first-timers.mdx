## Quick Answer

Beijing is **safe, structured, and rewarding**, but it is **rule-heavy**.  
If this is your first trip, visit Beijing **after Shanghai**, and **never arrive without reservations**.  
Most failures in Beijing happen because travelers underestimate **booking rules and security checks**.

---

## Why Beijing Is Different From Other Cities

Beijing is not hard because of language or transport.  
It is hard because:

- Major sights **require advance reservations**
- Security checks are frequent and slow
- Distances are large, even inside attractions
- Same-day improvisation often fails

If you follow the rules, Beijing works.  
If you improvise, you waste days.

---

## Exact Actions: How to Do Beijing Without Failing

### 1. Choose Beijing only if your trip length allows it
- **Minimum recommended stay:** 3 full days
- **Best combined with:** Shanghai (high contrast, lower stress)

If your schedule is tight or flexible days are limited, **do not start in Beijing**.

---

### 2. Book key attractions BEFORE arrival (non-negotiable)

For first-timers, these sites **require advance booking**:

- **Forbidden City**
- **Great Wall sections**
- **Some museums and historic complexes**

Rules to follow:
- Book several days ahead
- Use your passport exactly as shown
- Save booking confirmations as screenshots

If you do not have a booking, **you will not be allowed in**, even if you arrive early.

---

### 3. Plan your days around security checks

Expect security at:
- Metro station entrances
- Major tourist areas
- Large public squares

**What this means in practice:**
- Arrive early for first attractions
- Do not stack tight appointments
- Expect bag scans and ID checks

Security is normal, not personal.  
Arguing or asking “why” does not speed things up.

---

### 4. Stay close to a major metro line

Recommended first-timer base areas:
- **Dongcheng** (near historic core)
- **Chaoyang** (modern hotels, good metro access)

Avoid remote hotels even if cheaper.

**Rule:**  
If your hotel is more than **15 minutes walk** to a metro station, change hotels.

---

### 5. Use the metro, not taxis, for most movement

- Metro is predictable and faster during peak hours
- Signs include English
- Stations have staff and security presence

Activate Beijing **Transport** QR in Alipay.
If it fails, ticket machines work reliably.

---

### 6. Prepare payment and ID before queues

Before you enter any attraction:
- Open payment **before** you queue
- Keep your passport accessible but secure
- Confirm QR codes are moving

If payment or scanning fails:
- Step aside
- Switch method
- Rejoin the line

Do not troubleshoot while holding the queue.

---

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes

- **No reservation at an attraction**: you will be denied entry → move to another site.
- **Security line is longer than expected**: drop a secondary plan, not the main one.
- **Metro station temporarily closed**: follow staff instructions and reroute.
- **Payment fails at ticketing**: switch wallets or use cash immediately.
- **You feel rushed or confused**: step away and reset at a staffed area.

Most failures are caused by time pressure.

---

## Reality Check

- Beijing rewards preparation, not spontaneity.
- Security checks can add 30–60 minutes without warning.
- Large sites require more walking than expected.
- Missing one reservation can invalidate an entire day.

Plan fewer things, but lock them in.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Locals book attractions days ahead.
- Locals arrive early and leave buffer time.
- Locals change plans when security slows movement.
- Locals avoid cross-city travel during peak hours.

Do not fight the system—work with it.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] At least 3 full days allocated.
- [ ] Forbidden City / major sites booked in advance.
- [ ] Booking confirmations saved offline.
- [ ] Hotel within 15 minutes of a metro.
- [ ] Beijing Transport QR tested or ticket plan ready.
- [ ] Passport accessible but secure.
