## Quick Answer

If this is your **first trip to China**, start with **Shanghai**.  
It has the **lowest friction**, **best transport**, and **highest success rate** for foreigners.  
You can complete payments, transport, and daily movement here with minimal uncertainty.

If you choose only one city, choose **Shanghai**.

---

## Why Shanghai Works for First-Timers

Shanghai succeeds because it is **system-first**, not because it is “modern”.

It offers:
- Dense metro coverage across all major areas
- Clear English signage in airports and stations
- High acceptance of Alipay and WeChat Pay
- Hotels experienced with foreign guests
- Predictable airport-to-city transfers

Most first-trip failures (payment, navigation, lodging) are **least likely** here.

---

## Exact Actions: How to Set Up Shanghai Correctly

### 1. Choose the right arrival airport and transfer
- **Pudong (PVG)**: international arrivals, metro and airport train available
- **Hongqiao (SHA)**: closer to city center, also connected by metro

After arrival:
- Follow **Metro** signs, not taxi touts.
- Use metro or app-based rides (Didi inside Alipay / WeChat).

If a ride request or payment fails, step aside and retry—Shanghai staff expect this.

---

### 2. Stay in a first-timer-friendly area

Choose **one** of these base areas:

- **People’s Square** — central, multiple metro lines, easiest navigation
- **Jing’an** — good hotels, walkable, less crowded at night
- **Lujiazui** — modern, clear layout, easy airport transfers

Avoid staying far from metro stations.

**Rule:**  
If walking time to the nearest metro exceeds **15 minutes**, change hotels.

---

### 3. Set up payments before you move around
- Open Alipay or WeChat **Pay** before queuing.
- Confirm the QR code is **moving**.
- If payment fails, switch wallets or pay cash—do not troubleshoot in line.

Shanghai accepts mobile payments almost everywhere, but **speed matters**.

---

### 4. Use the metro as your default transport
- Metro signs and maps include English.
- Stations have staff and security presence.
- Fares are predictable and low.

Activate **Transport** QR in Alipay for Shanghai.
If it fails, ticket machines accept cash and cards.

---

### 5. Save addresses in Chinese
- Save your hotel name and address in **Chinese characters**.
- Screenshot the address.
- Show it to drivers or staff instead of typing.

This solves most navigation problems instantly.

---

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes

- **Payment fails at a counter**: step aside, restart the app, switch wallets, or pay cash.
- **Metro QR does not scan**: buy a ticket from the machine and continue.
- **Ride pickup is confusing**: cancel and reorder from a clear entrance.
- **Hotel is hard to find**: show the Chinese address to staff or drivers.
- **You feel overwhelmed**: pause at a mall or hotel lobby and reset.

Shanghai is forgiving if you slow down.

---

## Reality Check

- Shanghai is busy, but **structured**.
- Staff expect foreigners and are used to brief confusion.
- Cancelling, retrying, and stepping aside is normal.
- Most problems are solved faster by **changing method**, not fixing it.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Locals default to the metro because it avoids traffic and confusion.
- Locals cancel rides if pickup points are unclear.
- Locals switch payment methods immediately when something fails.
- Locals cluster activities to avoid unnecessary transfers.

Copy these habits.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Arrived via PVG or SHA with metro access.
- [ ] Hotel within 15 minutes of a metro station.
- [ ] Payment apps working and tested.
- [ ] Shanghai Transport QR activated or ticket plan ready.
- [ ] Hotel address saved in Chinese.
