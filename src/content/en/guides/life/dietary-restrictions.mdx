## Quick Answer

Dietary restrictions in China are **possible but limited**.  
Simple exclusions work best; complex or strict requirements are **hard to guarantee**.  
Your goal should be **risk reduction**, not perfect compliance.

---

## The Core Reality You Need to Accept

In most local restaurants:
- Ingredients are pre-prepared
- Sauces are mixed in advance
- Staff are focused on speed, not customization
- Cross-contact is common

Restaurants are not designed for **custom orders**.

---

## Restrictions That Are Usually Manageable

These are **low-risk** if handled correctly:

- No beef or no pork
- Mild spice preference
- Avoiding obvious shellfish
- Vegetarian dishes (not vegan)

Success depends on **choosing the right dishes**, not explaining rules.

---

## Restrictions That Are High Risk

These are **difficult to guarantee**:

- Strict vegan
- Severe food allergies
- Gluten-free
- Religious slaughter requirements

If your restriction is medically critical, **plan conservatively**.

---

## Exact Actions: How to Eat Safely With Restrictions

### 1. Choose dish types that fit your needs
Look for:
- Plain vegetable dishes
- Rice or noodle bowls with visible ingredients
- Steamed or stir-fried items without heavy sauces

Avoid:
- Mixed hotpots
- Saucy stews
- Dishes with unclear bases

---

### 2. Use simple, written exclusions
Prepare translated phrases such as:
- “No pork”
- “No beef”
- “No seafood”
- “Vegetarian only”

Show text, do not explain verbally.

---

### 3. Order fewer items first
- Order one dish
- Check it visually
- Adjust next order if needed

This reduces exposure risk.

---

### 4. Use safer dining environments when needed
For stricter needs:
- Hotel restaurants
- International chains
- Vegetarian-focused restaurants

These environments handle customization better.

---

## What Not to Do

- Do not explain medical or religious reasoning
- Do not ask staff to “guarantee” anything
- Do not expect ingredient separation
- Do not assume Western-style allergen awareness

Over-explaining increases confusion.

---

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes

- **Hidden meat appears**: stop eating and switch dishes.
- **Staff seems unsure**: choose a simpler item or another restaurant.
- **Language confusion**: point to vegetarian section only.
- **Cross-contact concerns**: eat packaged or chain food.
- **Strict allergy risk**: avoid local kitchens entirely.

Safety beats variety.

---

## Reality Check

- Even locals do not know full ingredient lists.
- Dishes change by region and cook.
- Flexibility improves outcomes.
- Control comes from choice, not explanation.

Your strategy matters more than the menu.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Locals avoid dishes they cannot identify.
- Locals adapt choices rather than customize.
- Locals eat elsewhere if unsure.
- Locals prioritize familiarity over novelty.

Follow the same logic.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Identify non-negotiable restrictions.
- [ ] Prepare short exclusion phrases.
- [ ] Choose visually simple dishes.
- [ ] Order in small steps.
- [ ] Have backup food options.
