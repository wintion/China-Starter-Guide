## Quick Answer

For short trips, apartments in China are usually **less convenient than they look**.

They make the most sense when:
- You are staying longer
- You can handle paperwork
- You are comfortable without hotel-style support

For many visitors, the “cheaper apartment” ends up costing more in time, friction, and uncertainty.

---

## Why Apartments Feel Harder Than Hotels

An apartment stay usually means:
- You handle accommodation registration more directly
- You depend on the landlord or host
- Access can be less smooth
- Delivery, repair, and check-in issues are more DIY
- Contracts or house rules may be only in Chinese

Hotels absorb a lot of this for you. Apartments do not.

---

## When Renting Starts To Make Sense

It becomes more reasonable if:
- You are staying at least several weeks
- You know the city already
- You need more space or kitchen access
- You have a stable routine, not a fast-moving itinerary

That is very different from a first trip across multiple cities.

---

## When a Hotel Is Usually the Better Choice

Prefer a hotel if:
- You are staying only a few nights or a week or two
- You are arriving late
- You do not speak or read Chinese confidently
- You want easy receipts, check-in, luggage handling, or support
- You may change plans during the trip

Many travelers underestimate how valuable a front desk is.

---

## What To Confirm Before Renting

### 1. Is the stay really suitable for foreigners?

Do not assume.  
Ask directly:
- Can foreigners stay here normally?
- How is accommodation registration handled?
- Who meets me at check-in?

If the answers are vague, that is already information.

---

### 2. What is the exact address?

Get the full Chinese address before arrival.  
Without that, taxis, deliveries, and registration all become harder.

---

### 3. What is the real check-in process?

Clarify:
- Who gives you the keys
- What time they are available
- Whether there is a code, gate, or building access issue
- What happens if you arrive late

“Self check-in” sounds easy until you are tired, outside, and locked out.

---

### 4. What are the money terms?

Make sure you understand:
- Deposit
- Cleaning fee
- Utilities
- Early checkout rules
- Damage responsibility

If the terms are unclear before you book, they will not become clearer later.

---

## The Registration Issue Matters

Hotels almost always handle registration for you.  
Apartments usually do not.

If your host treats registration like an annoying detail, take that seriously.  
It is often a sign the entire stay may be poorly managed.

---

## Practical Advice

- Use apartments for stable stays, not chaotic itineraries
- Keep one nearby hotel saved as a fallback
- Avoid arriving at a private rental very late on your first night in a city
- Do not sign or confirm anything you do not understand

Convenience is part of cost.

---

## Reality Check

- Apartments can be great for longer, calmer stays
- They are often a bad idea for first-time short-term travel
- Many visitors overestimate the savings
- A hotel can be the cheaper choice once friction is counted honestly

Choose the setup that matches your real trip, not the marketing photos.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] My stay is long enough to justify an apartment.
- [ ] I confirmed the exact Chinese address.
- [ ] I understand how check-in works.
- [ ] I know how accommodation registration will be handled.
- [ ] I understand the money terms and have a backup hotel.
