## Quick Answer

China’s visa-free policies are **real and useful**, but **strictly defined**.  
If you qualify, you can enter **without applying for a visa in advance**—but **only for specific purposes and durations**.  
Visa-free entry is **not flexible** and **not extendable by default**.

---

## The Core Reality (Why People Get This Wrong)

Most problems happen because travelers assume:
- “Visa-free” means flexible → **False**
- “I can extend after arrival” → **Usually false**
- “I can change purpose later” → **False**
- “Short stay rules are relaxed” → **False**

Visa-free is simple—but **rigid**.

---

## What “Visa-Free” Actually Means

Visa-free entry allows you to:
- Enter China without applying for a visa beforehand
- Stay **up to a fixed number of days**
- Travel for **tourism, business, or transit** (as defined)

It does **not** mean:
- Open-ended stay
- Work or study
- Automatic extensions
- Multiple entries unless specified

---

## Who Can Use Visa-Free Entry

Eligibility depends on:
- Your passport nationality
- Your point of entry
- The specific visa-free scheme

Always check **current eligibility** before travel—rules vary by passport.

---

## Length of Stay: Count Carefully

Key rules:
- Stay length is **strictly counted**
- Overstaying even one day matters
- Entry day often counts as Day 1
- Departure must be on or before the allowed day

Do not rely on assumptions—**count exactly**.

---

## What You Must Prepare for Visa-Free Entry

Even without a visa, you still need:
- A valid passport
- A completed arrival form (if required)
- Proof of onward or return travel
- Accommodation details
- Clear explanation of your purpose

Immigration officers may ask.

---

## Common Visa-Free Mistakes

- Booking return flights beyond allowed days
- Assuming extensions are easy
- Entering with vague travel plans
- Using visa-free entry for work-related activities
- Miscounting days

Most refusals are paperwork-related, not personal.

---

## Can You Extend a Visa-Free Stay?

In most cases:
- **No**
- Extensions are not guaranteed
- Approval is discretionary and rare

Plan your stay to fit the visa-free window.

---

## What Happens If You Overstay

Overstaying:
- Triggers fines
- Can cause future visa trouble
- May affect future entries

Do not test the system—it is enforced.

---

## Reality Check

- Visa-free entry is convenient, not forgiving
- Immigration rules are applied consistently
- Clear plans reduce questioning
- Preparation matters more than persuasion

Follow the rules exactly.

---

## What Locals (and Frequent Travelers) Do

- Confirm eligibility before booking
- Count days conservatively
- Leave buffer days
- Avoid last-day departures
- Do not rely on extensions

This avoids stress.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Confirm passport eligibility.
- [ ] Know your allowed stay length.
- [ ] Book return/onward travel within limits.
- [ ] Prepare accommodation details.
- [ ] Do not plan extensions.
