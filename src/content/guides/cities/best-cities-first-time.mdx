## Quick Answer
Start with one or two major cities and add one culture focused stop. This keeps travel time low and helps you settle in.

## Why this matters
Too many cities can turn a trip into constant transit. A focused list gives you time to explore and rest.

## Step-by-step
1. Choose a primary city that matches your interests: history, food, or modern skyline.
2. Add one contrasting city to diversify your experience.
3. Check travel time between cities and avoid long back to back transfers.
4. Pick areas to stay near central transport hubs to reduce daily travel time.
5. Verify locally for ticket rules or city entry requirements.

## Common problems & fixes
- Itinerary feels rushed: remove one city and add rest days.
- Long transfers drain your energy: switch to a closer city pair.
- Too many day trips planned: keep one day open for flexibility.

## Checklist
- [ ] Primary city chosen for your top interest.
- [ ] Secondary city adds contrast, not overlap.
- [ ] Travel times checked and reasonable.
- [ ] Areas to stay identified near transit.
