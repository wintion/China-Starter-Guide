## Quick Answer
Short trips work best with one or two cities. Longer trips can handle three or four if transfers are short.

## Why this matters
Every city change costs time for packing, transit, and orientation. Fewer moves means deeper experiences.

## Step-by-step
1. Estimate how many full days you want in each city.
2. Count travel days as partial days and reduce activity plans on those days.
3. Choose nearby city pairs connected by fast rail when possible.
4. Add a buffer day for rest or delays.
5. Verify locally for transport schedules during peak periods.

## Common problems & fixes
- Too many transfers: remove one city and add a day trip instead.
- Early burnout: schedule lighter days after long travel.
- Missed sights: extend stays in your top city.

## Checklist
- [ ] Full days per city planned.
- [ ] Travel days treated as lighter days.
- [ ] Buffer day added.
- [ ] City pair distances checked.
