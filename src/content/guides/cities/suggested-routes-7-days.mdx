## Quick Answer
Pick two nearby cities with fast rail links. Aim for three to four nights in the first city and the rest in the second.

## Why this matters
A one week trip is short. A tight route reduces time lost to transfers.

## Step-by-step
1. Choose a primary city for arrival and major sights.
2. Add one nearby city connected by high speed rail.
3. Allocate most nights to the primary city and fewer nights to the second.
4. Keep travel day light with only one major activity.
5. Verify locally for ticketed attractions that require reservations.

## Common problems & fixes
- Too many attractions planned: cut one and focus on top priorities.
- Train tickets sold out: shift the travel day or switch city order.
- Late arrival fatigue: schedule a relaxed first evening.

## Checklist
- [ ] Two city route selected.
- [ ] Rail times confirmed.
- [ ] Travel day kept light.
- [ ] Reservation needs checked.
