## Quick Answer
Summer can be hot and humid in many regions. Plan early starts, take breaks, and stay hydrated.

## Why this matters
Heat affects your pace and comfort. A smart schedule prevents burnout and keeps trips enjoyable.

## Step-by-step
1. Start outdoor sightseeing early and save indoor visits for mid day.
2. Carry water and light snacks for long walks.
3. Use metro or taxis to reduce walking during the hottest hours.
4. Watch for heavy rain and build flexible plans.
5. Verify locally for seasonal closures or weather alerts.

## Common problems & fixes
- Heat exhaustion: take breaks in air conditioned places and hydrate.
- Sudden rain: carry a compact umbrella or rain jacket.
- Crowded attractions: visit early or choose less busy sites.

## Checklist
- [ ] Early start planned.
- [ ] Water and sun protection ready.
- [ ] Indoor backup activities selected.
- [ ] Weather alerts checked.
