## Quick Answer
English menus are common in tourist areas but not guaranteed. Use translation tools and photos as backup.

## Why this matters
Relying only on English menus can slow you down. A backup plan keeps meals easy.

## Step-by-step
1. Ask politely if an English menu is available.
2. If not, use camera translation for key items.
3. Point to photos or sample dishes if available.
4. Confirm any allergies or spice levels with short notes.
5. Verify locally if ingredients are unclear.

## Common problems & fixes
- No English menu: use translation apps and point to items.
- Mixed language menu: confirm key ingredients and portion size.
- Menu changes: ask for the most popular dish.

## Checklist
- [ ] Translation app open before ordering.
- [ ] Allergy notes ready.
- [ ] Photos used to confirm dish.
- [ ] Payment method ready.
