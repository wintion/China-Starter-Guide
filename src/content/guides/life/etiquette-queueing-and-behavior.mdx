## Quick Answer
Queues can move quickly and patterns vary by place. Stay calm, follow local cues, and keep space for others.

## Why this matters
Understanding local habits reduces friction in stations, shops, and restaurants.

## Step-by-step
1. Watch how the local queue is formed and follow the pattern.
2. Keep your items close and avoid blocking walkways.
3. Let people exit trains or elevators before entering.
4. Use a calm tone when asking for help or clarification.
5. Verify locally for venue specific rules.

## Common problems & fixes
- Queue feels unclear: ask staff where to stand.
- Someone cuts in line: stay calm and move to a clear spot.
- Crowded space: wait to board until the exit flow finishes.

## Checklist
- [ ] Local queue pattern observed.
- [ ] Calm tone used in busy areas.
- [ ] Walkways kept clear.
- [ ] Patience and flexibility ready.
