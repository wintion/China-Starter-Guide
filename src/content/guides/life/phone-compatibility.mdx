## Quick Answer

Most modern phones work in China, but **many fail in subtle ways**.  
Problems usually come from **SIM setup, band support, battery behavior, and system restrictions**, not from the phone “not being supported.”  
Check and prepare **before you arrive**, not after something breaks.

---

## The Core Reality (Why “It Has Signal” Isn’t Enough)

In China, a phone can show signal and still:
- Load maps slowly
- Fail QR payments
- Miss verification messages
- Drop data in busy areas
- Drain battery unusually fast

Compatibility is about **reliability under load**, not basic connection.

---

## Phones That Work Best (General Guidance)

Phones with the **highest success rate**:
- Recent flagship models
- Devices with strong LTE/5G band support
- Phones with good battery health
- Dual-SIM capable phones (physical SIM preferred)

Phones that need extra care:
- Older models
- Import variants with limited bands
- Devices running aggressive battery optimization

---

## Exact Actions: Prepare Your Phone Properly

### 1. Check band compatibility (do this once)
- Confirm your phone supports common LTE/5G bands used locally
- If unsure, expect slower speeds in crowded areas
- This matters most for **older or region-specific models**

If your phone is several years old, plan conservatively.

---

### 2. Set up dual SIM correctly (if available)
Recommended setup:
- **Slot 1:** local physical SIM (data)
- **Slot 2:** home SIM (SMS only, roaming off for data)

Disable data switching to avoid accidental roaming charges.

---

### 3. Disable aggressive battery restrictions
Some systems limit background activity.

Before arrival:
- Allow background activity for:
  - Payment apps
  - Maps
  - Messaging apps
- Disable extreme battery-saving modes

If apps freeze or notifications lag, battery settings are often the cause.

---

### 4. Update the system and apps before you travel
- Install OS updates
- Update payment, map, and messaging apps
- Restart once after updates

Do **not** update systems mid-trip unless necessary.

---

### 5. Prepare for QR-heavy usage
QR scanning requires:
- Camera access
- Screen brightness
- Stable data

Check:
- Camera permissions enabled
- Screen brightness adjustable quickly
- No overlays blocking QR display

These small issues cause real-world delays.

---

## Common Compatibility Traps

- **“Brand-new phone, new OS”**: apps may not be fully optimized yet.
- **Enterprise or work-managed phones**: restrictions may block payments or QR scanning.
- **Imported models**: missing bands reduce reliability in dense areas.
- **Low battery health**: throttling affects data and camera use.

Newer is not always safer.

---

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes

- **Signal present but apps hang**: disable battery saving and retry.
- **QR code won’t scan**: increase brightness and clean the camera lens.
- **Payments fail intermittently**: switch networks or SIM.
- **Messages delayed**: allow background data and notifications.
- **Phone overheats or drains fast**: reduce background apps and screen brightness.

Fix settings before blaming the network.

---

## Reality Check

- Most failures are configuration issues.
- Phones are used more intensively in China than expected.
- Small settings cause outsized problems.
- Preparation saves time at counters and gates.

Your phone is your ticket, wallet, and map—treat it as critical equipment.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Locals use stable phone models.
- Locals avoid changing phone settings during trips.
- Locals carry power banks.
- Locals fix issues early, not in queues.

Follow their habits.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Band support checked or risk accepted.
- [ ] Dual SIM configured correctly.
- [ ] Battery restrictions disabled for key apps.
- [ ] System and apps updated before travel.
- [ ] Camera and QR permissions tested.
- [ ] Power bank packed.
