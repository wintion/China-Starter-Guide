## Quick Answer

For most first-time visitors, **use a physical SIM card, not eSIM**.  
eSIMs are **less compatible, harder to troubleshoot, and easier to misconfigure** in China.  
If connectivity matters for payments, navigation, and verification, **physical SIM wins**.

---

## The Reality: Why eSIM Is Not China-Friendly

Despite marketing claims, eSIMs in China face practical issues:

- Limited carrier compatibility
- Inconsistent performance across regions
- Harder APN troubleshooting
- Poor support at counters and hotels
- Higher failure rate for QR payments and ride apps

When an eSIM fails, **you are usually on your own**.

---

## What Physical SIM Cards Do Better

Physical SIMs offer:
- Stable local carrier integration
- Predictable speeds and latency
- Easier troubleshooting by staff
- Better compatibility with payment apps
- Reliable SMS reception when enabled

They are **boring, but reliable**—which is exactly what you want.

---

## When eSIM Might Be Acceptable (Narrow Cases)

eSIM can work **only if all are true**:

- Your phone is confirmed eSIM-compatible
- You do not need SMS verification
- You are comfortable adjusting APN settings
- You accept that support is minimal
- You have offline backups ready

If any condition fails, **do not use eSIM**.

---

## Exact Actions: Choose the Right Option

### Option A: Physical SIM (Recommended)
Choose this if:
- This is your first trip to China
- You rely on mobile payments
- You need stable maps and ride-hailing
- You want easy fixes at the airport

**Action:** Buy from official airport counters or major carriers.

---

### Option B: eSIM (Use With Caution)
Choose this only if:
- You have used eSIMs successfully before
- You have a backup plan
- You are comfortable with technical setup

**Action:** Test activation and data **before departure**.

---

## Setup Rules That Prevent Failure

### For Physical SIM
- Insert and test data at the counter
- Load a map and a website before leaving
- Confirm data speed is usable
- Keep your home SIM if you need SMS

---

### For eSIM
- Activate before you fly
- Confirm APN settings manually if needed
- Test payments, maps, and messaging
- Screenshot setup details
- Carry a backup SIM plan

Never assume “it will just work.”

---

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes

- **eSIM shows signal but no data**: manual APN setup or switch to physical SIM.
- **Payment apps fail on eSIM**: switch network or change SIM.
- **Cannot receive verification SMS**: reinsert home SIM.
- **Airport staff cannot help with eSIM**: buy a physical SIM immediately.
- **Connectivity drops in smaller cities**: physical SIM performs better.

Troubleshooting eSIMs wastes travel time.

---

## Reality Check

- eSIM is convenient elsewhere, not here.
- Physical SIMs are still the local standard.
- Support staff understand SIMs, not eSIMs.
- Stability matters more than elegance.

Choose reliability over novelty.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Locals use physical SIMs.
- Locals keep one number stable.
- Locals avoid changing network setups mid-trip.
- Locals fix issues in person, not via apps.

Copy the stable approach.
