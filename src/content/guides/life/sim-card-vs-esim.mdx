## Quick Answer
Use a physical SIM if your phone is unlocked and supports it. Use eSIM for faster setup if your device supports eSIM.

## Why this matters
Connectivity powers payments, maps, and messaging. Choosing the right option prevents downtime.

## Step-by-step
1. Check your phone settings for eSIM support and SIM lock status.
2. Decide based on convenience, price, and data needs.
3. If using a SIM, plan where to buy it and bring your passport.
4. If using eSIM, install the profile before arrival and test data.
5. Verify locally for any registration steps or limits.

### Cautions
- Do not remove your home SIM if you need it for verification texts.
- Keep QR codes and activation details secure.
- Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive setup steps.

## Common problems & fixes
- No signal after activation: restart the phone and confirm APN settings.
- eSIM profile fails: re-scan the QR code or re-download the profile.
- SIM not working: confirm your phone is unlocked.

## Checklist
- [ ] Phone unlocked and compatible.
- [ ] Activation steps saved offline.
- [ ] Passport ready for registration.
- [ ] Backup connectivity plan.
