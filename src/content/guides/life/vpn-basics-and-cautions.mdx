## Quick Answer

Do not rely on a single connection method.  
In China, **some services will fail intermittently even when tools show “Connected”**.  
Your goal is not perfect access, but **task completion**.  
If something does not load within seconds, **stop retrying and switch to your backup plan**.

---

## The One Rule That Prevents Most Failures

**Never troubleshoot connectivity in real-time situations.**

If a service does not load:
- Switch once.
- If it still fails, **move on using offline or local alternatives**.

Repeated retries waste battery, time, and attention.

---

## Exact Actions: How to Stay Functional

### 1. Identify what must work (before you travel)

Open **Notes** and list only **critical services**, such as:
- Email for bookings or work
- Maps and navigation
- Payment confirmations
- Work authentication tools

If a service is optional, **do not depend on it**.

This list defines what you must protect.

---

### 2. Prepare offline access for every critical item

For each critical service, prepare an offline fallback:

- Download **offline maps** for arrival cities.
- Save **hotel names and addresses in Chinese** in **Notes**.
- Save **tickets, bookings, and QR codes** in **Photos** or **Files**.
- Screenshot anything you may need to show quickly.

If it cannot be accessed offline, **assume it may fail**.

---

### 3. Install and test connection tools before departure

If you use any connection or routing tool:

- Install and log in **before** traveling.
- Confirm you see clear indicators such as:
  - **Connected** status
  - A key or lock icon
  - A running session timer

**Important:**  
If the tool shows **Connected** but apps do not load, treat it as **not working**.

Do not debug on the spot—switch plans.

---

### 4. Set a fast switch rule (no exceptions)

Configure quick access to:
- **Wi-Fi**
- **Cellular Data**

Use this rule:
- If an app does not load within **10 seconds**, switch networks once.
- If it still fails, **stop and use offline notes or local apps**.

Do not toggle repeatedly.

---

### 5. Use local alternatives for time-sensitive tasks

For anything time-sensitive:
- Use local map or ride apps instead of waiting.
- Ask **hotel or station staff** to check information.
- Show saved addresses or screenshots rather than reloading apps.

In China, **staffed counters resolve problems faster than apps**.

---

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes

- **Tool shows “Connected” but nothing loads**: treat as failed; switch to offline or local apps.
- **App hangs on login**: network restriction or outage; stop retrying.
- **Verification SMS does not arrive**: use backup codes or switch accounts.
- **Battery drains quickly**: disable background refresh and use offline tools.
- **Hotel Wi-Fi blocks services**: switch to mobile data or ask staff for directions.
- **Critical task blocked**: move to a staffed desk instead of retrying.

---

## Reality Check

- Your email will not load at hotel check-in; show the booking screenshot.
- A map app freezes in a taxi; show the pinned Chinese address from **Notes**.
- A connection tool fails twice; stop and continue offline.
- Battery drops quickly after repeated retries; switch to offline mode early.

Finishing the task matters more than restoring access.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Locals stop retrying quickly and switch tools.
- Locals save addresses and screenshots because they are faster to show.
- Locals ask staff when apps fail because staff have authority and context.
- Locals preserve battery because connectivity can be inconsistent on the move.

Copy these behaviors.

---

## Checklist (Before You Leave)

- [ ] Critical services listed.
- [ ] Offline maps downloaded.
- [ ] Hotel and key addresses saved in Chinese.
- [ ] Tickets and confirmations saved as screenshots.
- [ ] Connection tools installed and logged in.
- [ ] Quick network switch enabled.
