## Quick Answer

Public Wi-Fi in China is **unreliable and restricted**.  
You should **never depend on it** for navigation, payments, or verification codes.  
Your primary internet must be **mobile data**, with offline backups ready.

Most frustration comes from assuming Wi-Fi works “like other countries”.

---

## The Core Reality (No Sugarcoating)

In China:

- Public Wi-Fi often requires **local phone verification**
- Many networks block or throttle international services
- Hotel Wi-Fi quality varies widely
- Login portals may fail in English
- Speeds are inconsistent even when connected

Wi-Fi exists—but it is **not a dependable backbone**.

---

## Where Public Wi-Fi Exists (and Its Limits)

### Common Places With Wi-Fi
- Hotels
- Airports
- Cafés and malls
- High-speed trains

### Common Problems
- SMS verification required
- Frequent disconnects
- Login pages not loading
- Limited access to email or maps

**Connected ≠ usable.**

---

## Exact Actions: How to Stay Online Without Stress

### 1. Treat mobile data as your primary internet
- Use a local SIM or eSIM
- Assume Wi-Fi is secondary
- Keep mobile data enabled by default

If mobile data fails, **switch locations**, not networks.

---

### 2. Do not rely on Wi-Fi for critical tasks
Never depend on Wi-Fi for:
- Payment confirmations
- Ride-hailing
- Navigation while moving
- SMS or verification codes

Complete these tasks on mobile data only.

---

### 3. Prepare offline access in advance
Before you arrive:
- Download offline maps
- Save hotel addresses in Chinese
- Screenshot tickets and bookings
- Save emergency contacts locally

Offline access is not optional—it is insurance.

---

### 4. Use hotel Wi-Fi only after check-in
Hotel Wi-Fi is best used for:
- Updates
- Backups
- Longer sessions

If hotel Wi-Fi is slow:
- Switch to mobile data
- Do not troubleshoot at the desk unless necessary

---

### 5. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive logins
- Banking
- Account recovery
- Password resets

Use mobile data instead.

---

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes

- **Wi-Fi asks for SMS code you cannot receive**: switch to mobile data.
- **Connected but nothing loads**: disconnect and move on.
- **Login page stuck in Chinese**: abandon and use data.
- **Wi-Fi drops during payment**: stop and retry on mobile data only.
- **Airport Wi-Fi fails after landing**: use mobile data immediately.

Do not fight broken networks.

---

## Reality Check

- Wi-Fi is not designed for foreigners.
- Connectivity varies by city and building.
- Mobile data is faster and more reliable.
- Offline access saves entire days.

Success comes from **not expecting Wi-Fi to work**.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Locals rely on mobile data.
- Locals use Wi-Fi only as a bonus.
- Locals screenshot everything important.
- Locals switch locations instead of networks.

You should do the same.
