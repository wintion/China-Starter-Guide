## Quick Answer
Public Wi-Fi can be useful, but use it for low-risk tasks and avoid sensitive logins.

## Why this matters
Many cafes and hotels offer Wi-Fi, but public networks can be insecure.

## Step-by-step
1. Ask staff for the official network name and password.
2. Connect and open a simple site to confirm access.
3. Use public Wi-Fi for maps or browsing, not banking.
4. Switch to mobile data for sensitive actions.
5. Verify locally for any login requirements or captive portals.

### Cautions
- Avoid entering passwords on unknown networks.
- Turn off auto-join for public Wi-Fi after use.
- Use a trusted network for account recovery or payments.

## Common problems & fixes
- Network requires phone verification: use your SIM data instead.
- Wi-Fi is slow: move closer to the router or switch to data.
- Captive portal not loading: try opening a non-HTTPS page to trigger it.

## Checklist
- [ ] Official network name confirmed.
- [ ] Sensitive actions kept to mobile data.
- [ ] Auto-join disabled after use.
- [ ] Backup connectivity ready.
