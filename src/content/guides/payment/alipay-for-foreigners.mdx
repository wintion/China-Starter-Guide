## Quick Answer
Install Alipay, complete passport verification, and bind a foreign card before you try to pay. At checkout you either **Scan** the merchant code or show your **Pay** code from **Pay/Collect**; if one fails, switch flows or switch wallets immediately. Do a small test payment first so you do not discover errors in a live queue.

## Exact Actions
1. Install Alipay and set the app language.
   - Open **App Store** or **Google Play**, install **Alipay**, then open the app.
   - Tap **Me** (bottom right) > **Settings** > **General** > **Language** > **English**.
   - If **Language** is missing, the app build is outdated or the menu did not load; update Alipay in the store, relaunch, and check **Me** > **Settings** > **General** again.
2. Complete identity verification (mandatory for reliable payment).
   - Tap **Me** > **Settings** > **Account and Security** > **Identity Verification** > **Passport**.
   - Follow the prompts to upload your passport photo page and enter your name exactly as shown.
   - If **Identity Verification** does not appear, your account is not fully initialized or the build is outdated; update the app, relaunch, and retry on stable data.
3. Bind a foreign card.
   - Tap **Me** > **Wallet** > **Cards** > **Add card**, then enter card number, expiry, and CVV.
   - Use the same name order as your passport and confirm any verification prompts.
   - If **Add card** fails, your issuer is blocking wallet binding or your name does not match; call your bank to allow international wallet usage, then retry or switch to another card.
4. Locate the two payment entry points.
   - On the Alipay home screen, tap **Scan** to scan a merchant QR.
   - Tap **Pay/Collect** > **Pay** to display your personal payment code for a cashier to scan.
   - If **Pay/Collect** or **Pay** is missing, your wallet is not fully enabled; complete identity verification and ensure at least one card is bound, then restart the app.
5. Make a test payment before relying on Alipay.
   - In a convenience store, tap **Scan**, scan the merchant QR, confirm the merchant name, and tap **Pay**.
   - If the merchant name looks wrong, the code is for another shop or a personal transfer; cancel, ask for the official QR, and rescan.
6. Enable Transport QR for metro and buses.
   - Tap **Transport** on the home screen, select your city, and accept the activation prompts.
   - You will see a black-and-white transit QR code specific to that city.
   - If **Transport** is missing or activation fails, your verification may be incomplete or the city is not supported; finish identity verification and, if the city still does not appear, use ticket machines or local transit apps as a backup.

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes
- **Payment shows “Transaction not allowed”**: the merchant terminal does not accept foreign cards or your card is blocked for wallet use; switch to another wallet or pay cash, then call your bank to enable wallet transactions.
- **Payment stuck on “Processing”**: network is unstable and the terminal timed out; check **Bills** for the final status before retrying to avoid double charge.
- **Cashier scans your code and nothing happens**: screen brightness is too low or the scanner is dirty; increase brightness, hold the phone steady, and if it still fails, use **Scan** on the merchant code instead.
- **Scan opens the camera but never detects the QR**: glare or damaged code; move closer, tilt the phone, or ask for a printed QR.
- **Card added but payments still fail**: your card issuer allows binding but blocks merchant categories; test at a different merchant and keep a backup payment method ready.

## Reality Check
- Some counters accept only WeChat Pay and will not try a second time with Alipay.
- Payment can work in one city and fail in another without explanation.
- Alipay menus can change after updates, and features can disappear until verification completes.
- The cashier often expects you to show your **Pay** code, not scan theirs.

## What Locals Do Instead
- They switch to WeChat Pay immediately without debate.
- They pay via a friend and transfer later.
- They keep small cash notes for places that refuse QR or take too long to scan.
- They move to a different counter when a terminal is failing.

## Checklist
- [ ] Alipay installed and set to English.
- [ ] Identity verification completed with passport.
- [ ] At least one foreign card bound successfully.
- [ ] **Scan** and **Pay/Collect** located and tested.
- [ ] A successful test payment completed.
- [ ] Transport QR activated for your city.
- [ ] Backup cash ready.
