## Quick Answer
Choose one wallet as your primary and set up the other as backup. Both use QR codes, but availability can vary by merchant.

## Why this matters
Having both reduces the chance of being stuck if one wallet fails or a merchant only accepts the other.

## Step-by-step
1. Install both apps and complete identity verification while you have stable internet.
2. Open each app and find the Scan and Pay buttons so you know where they live.
3. Add a foreign card to each wallet so you can compare which one works best for you.
4. Test each wallet at a low-stakes purchase to confirm the flow and success message.
5. Decide which wallet you will open first at checkout.
6. Keep cash as a fallback for edge cases or smaller vendors.

## Common problems & fixes
- One wallet works and the other fails: keep the working one as primary and use cash as backup.
- QR codes not accepted: ask if the merchant prefers scan or show-to-pay.
- Screens or labels change: follow in-app guidance and verify locally.

## Checklist
- [ ] Both wallets installed and verified.
- [ ] Card added to each wallet.
- [ ] Primary wallet chosen.
- [ ] Backup method ready.
