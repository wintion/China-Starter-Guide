## Quick Answer
Most short-term visitors do not need a Chinese bank account. A verified wallet with a foreign card and some cash is usually enough.

## Why this matters
Opening a local account can take time and requires local documentation. Knowing if you really need one prevents wasted effort.

## Step-by-step
1. List your expected payment needs: daily purchases, transport, and major bookings.
2. Open your wallet app and check Wallet or Cards to confirm your foreign card is active.
3. Try a wallet payment on a small purchase before deciding on a bank account.
4. If you need a local account for long stays or special services, prepare local ID requirements and verify locally.
5. Keep a backup method for the transition period if you do open an account.

## Common problems & fixes
- Wallet limits feel too low: use a backup card or cash, then consider a local account.
- Bank requirements change: check with the branch in person and verify locally.
- Account opening takes time: plan for several visits and keep receipts.

## Checklist
- [ ] Wallet works with a foreign card.
- [ ] Backup cash and card ready.
- [ ] Decision made based on trip length and needs.
- [ ] Local requirements confirmed if opening an account.
