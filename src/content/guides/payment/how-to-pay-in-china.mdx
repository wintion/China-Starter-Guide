## Quick Answer

China is **not fully cashless**, but **mobile payment is the default**.  
As a foreigner, you should **set up Alipay first**, optionally WeChat Pay second, and **carry a small amount of cash as backup**.  
If you arrive without mobile payment ready, you will struggle with taxis, metro exits, small shops, and food ordering.

---

## Exact Actions: How to Pay in China as a Foreigner

This section assumes **you have a smartphone, a passport, and an international credit/debit card**.

### Step 1: Install Alipay (Before Arrival if Possible)

1. Open **App Store / Google Play**
2. Search for **“Alipay”** (publisher: Ant Group)
3. Install and open the app
4. Select **English** when prompted

What you should see:
- A blue home screen with “Scan”, “Pay”, “Transport”, “Tour Pass” (wording may vary)

If the app shows Chinese only:
- Tap the **profile icon (top right)** → Language → English

---

### Step 2: Verify Your Identity (Mandatory)

1. Tap **“Me”** (bottom right)
2. Tap **“Account & Security”**
3. Tap **“Identity Information”**
4. Choose **Passport**
5. Upload passport photo page
6. Enter your name **exactly as shown on the passport**
   - Order matters
   - Capitalization usually does not, but spelling must match

**Do not skip this.**  
Without identity verification:
- Card binding may fail
- Payment limits will be lower
- Some merchants will reject payment silently

---

### Step 3: Add a Foreign Bank Card

1. Go to **Me → Bank Cards**
2. Tap **“Add Card”**
3. Choose **International Card**
4. Enter card number, expiry date, CVV
5. Use your **passport name**, not nickname

Cards that usually work:
- Visa
- Mastercard
- Some debit cards with international payment enabled

Cards that often fail:
- Cards requiring SMS OTP to a blocked roaming number
- Corporate cards with strict MCC rules

---

### Step 4: Make Your First Test Payment

Before you rely on it:
1. Go to **Scan**
2. Scan any merchant QR code (even a convenience store)
3. Pay a **small amount** (e.g. water bottle)

If payment succeeds:
- You are mostly ready for daily life

If it fails:
- See “Failure Scenarios & Fixes” below

---

## Using WeChat Pay: When and Why

WeChat Pay is widely used, but **setup for foreigners is less forgiving**.

Use WeChat Pay if:
- You already use WeChat to communicate
- A merchant only shows WeChat QR codes
- You are splitting bills with Chinese friends

Do **not** rely on WeChat Pay as your only payment method unless:
- You have already tested it successfully in China

## Failure Scenarios & Fixes (Very Common)

### Payment Fails with “Transaction Not Allowed”

Likely reasons:
- Merchant does not support international cards
- Payment amount exceeds limit

What to do:
- Try Alipay balance or Tour Pass (if available)
- Ask the cashier: **“Can I use cash?”**
- Use a nearby convenience store to break cash

---

### Card Binding Fails Repeatedly

Common causes:
- Name order mismatch
- Bank blocks overseas wallet usage

Fix:
- Re-enter name exactly as passport
- Call your bank and ask to **allow overseas digital wallet payments**
- Try a different card if possible

---

### Metro Gate Rejects Payment

What surprises many foreigners:
- Metro systems often require **Transport QR**, not normal “Scan”

Fix:
1. Open Alipay
2. Tap **Transport**
3. Select the city
4. Use the **dedicated metro QR code**

---

## Reality Check: What Surprises Most Foreigners

- “Cash accepted” does not mean “cash convenient”
- Some small shops accept **only QR codes**
- Payment may work in one city but fail in another
- Staff often **cannot explain why payment failed**

This is normal. Do not panic.

---

## What Locals Do When Payment Doesn’t Work

- Switch apps instantly (Alipay ↔ WeChat)
- Ask a friend to pay and transfer later
- Walk to a convenience store that definitely accepts everything
- Carry **50–200 RMB** as a universal fallback

You should do the same.

---

## Checklist: Payment Readiness Before Day 1

- [ ] Alipay installed and verified
- [ ] At least one foreign card bound successfully
- [ ] Test payment completed
- [ ] Transport QR code activated
- [ ] Small amount of cash carried

## FAQ

**Do I need a Chinese bank account?**  
No. Most travelers do not need one.

**Is cash illegal or discouraged?**  
No. It is legal everywhere, but often inconvenient.

**Which app should I open first in line?**  
Alipay. It has higher international card acceptance.
