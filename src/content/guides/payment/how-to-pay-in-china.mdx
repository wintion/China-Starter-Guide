## Quick Answer
Most everyday payments run through mobile wallets. Keep a small amount of cash and a backup card, and set up at least one wallet before your first purchase.

## Why this matters
Payment habits are different across cities and merchants. If your primary method fails, having a second option prevents delays at checkout.

## Step-by-step
1. Decide on a primary wallet (Alipay or WeChat Pay) and a backup method (cash or card).
2. Install the wallet app and complete identity checks. Screens and labels can change, so verify locally if the flow looks different.
3. Add a foreign card and confirm any test charges or verification prompts.
4. Keep a small amount of cash for small shops, markets, or technical failures.
5. At checkout, look for a QR code to scan, or show your payment code if the clerk asks.

## Common problems & fixes
- Wallet setup fails: retry on stable Wi-Fi or mobile data, and confirm your phone number can receive verification codes.
- QR scan does not work: increase screen brightness, or ask to pay by showing your code instead.
- Merchant asks for cash only: pay with cash and note the location for later planning.

## Checklist
- [ ] Wallet app installed and verified.
- [ ] Foreign card added and working.
- [ ] Backup cash in small notes.
- [ ] Backup card accessible.
