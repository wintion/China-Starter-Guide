## Quick Answer
Tipping is not standard in most places. It may be accepted in some tours or hotels, so ask politely.

## Why this matters
Unnecessary tipping can cause confusion, but being prepared helps you respond in the few cases it is expected.

## Step-by-step
1. Check the receipt for any service charge before considering a tip.
2. If you want to tip, ask if it is accepted and how to do it.
3. In ride-hailing or delivery apps, look for a Tip button after the service and follow the on-screen prompt.
4. Use cash for small tips only when the staff confirms it is accepted.
5. Keep the exchange simple and respectful.
6. If staff refuse, thank them and do not insist.

## Common problems & fixes
- Tip refused: accept the refusal and move on.
- Unsure if a charge is included: ask the staff or check the receipt wording.
- App does not show a tip option: use cash only if the staff confirms it is accepted.

## Checklist
- [ ] Receipt checked for service charge.
- [ ] Staff asked if tipping is accepted.
- [ ] Tip amount kept modest.
- [ ] Cash on hand if needed.
