## Quick Answer
Keep conversations respectful and avoid sensitive topics in casual settings. Listen more and ask polite, neutral questions.

## Why this matters
Cultural misunderstandings can create awkward moments. A neutral approach keeps interactions friendly.

## Step-by-step
1. Focus on neutral topics like food, travel, and daily life.
2. Avoid pushing for opinions on sensitive political or historical issues.
3. If a conversation feels tense, change the topic politely.
4. Follow the lead of locals and match the tone of the conversation.
5. Verify locally for any setting specific etiquette.

## Common problems & fixes
- Conversation becomes tense: switch to neutral topics and thank the person.
- You are asked about sensitive issues: respond briefly and move on.
- Cultural misunderstanding: apologize and clarify your intent.

## Checklist
- [ ] Neutral topics prepared.
- [ ] Tone kept polite and calm.
- [ ] Respectful exit phrases ready.
- [ ] Local cues observed.
