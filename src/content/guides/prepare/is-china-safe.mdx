## Quick Answer
China is generally safe for visitors, but you should plan for common travel risks like scams, petty theft, and language barriers.

## Why this matters
Feeling confident about safety helps you make better decisions, especially in unfamiliar neighborhoods or late at night.

## Step-by-step
1. Check current travel advisories for your passport country and verify locally for updates.
2. Choose accommodations in well reviewed areas near transit lines.
3. Keep your phone charged and save your hotel address in Chinese.
4. Use official transport and avoid unmarked taxis.
5. Share your daily plan with a friend or travel companion when possible.

## Common problems & fixes
- You feel unsafe in a crowded area: move to a well lit spot and head to a main road.
- You lose your sense of direction: open your map app and return to a known landmark.
- A situation feels unclear: leave the area and seek help from staff or police.

## Checklist
- [ ] Hotel address saved in Chinese and English.
- [ ] Official transport apps ready.
- [ ] Emergency contacts stored offline.
- [ ] Simple daily plan with check ins.
