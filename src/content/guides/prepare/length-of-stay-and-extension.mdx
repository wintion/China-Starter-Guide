## Quick Answer
Your allowed stay depends on your entry type. Track your dates and confirm extension rules before your trip.

## Why this matters
Overstaying can lead to fines or travel issues. A clear plan helps you avoid accidental overstays.

## Step-by-step
1. Check your permitted stay based on your entry status and verify locally.
2. Mark your final allowed day on a calendar and add a buffer.
3. If you might stay longer, research extension options early.
4. Keep proof of entry date and travel plans handy.
5. If plans change, contact local authorities or official services for guidance.

## Common problems & fixes
- Confusing day count: use a calendar and confirm your entry date.
- Last minute extension need: seek guidance early and prepare documents.
- Overstay risk: change flights or exit before the deadline.

## Checklist
- [ ] Allowed stay confirmed.
- [ ] Final day marked with buffer.
- [ ] Extension options researched.
- [ ] Entry records saved.
