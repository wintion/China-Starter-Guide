## Quick Answer
Bring only what you need, keep prescriptions and labels, and verify restrictions before traveling.

## Why this matters
Some medicines are restricted or require documentation. Preparation prevents confiscation or delays.

## Step-by-step
1. List all medicines you plan to bring and check restrictions.
2. Keep medicines in original packaging with clear labels.
3. Carry a prescription or doctor note in English if possible.
4. Pack a reasonable personal use quantity and avoid bulk supplies.
5. Verify locally for any updated rules before departure.

## Common problems & fixes
- Medication questioned at customs: show labels and your prescription.
- Medicine lost in luggage: keep essential doses in your carry on.
- You need a refill: ask a local pharmacy or clinic with your prescription details.

## Checklist
- [ ] Restrictions checked for each medicine.
- [ ] Original packaging and labels kept.
- [ ] Prescription note packed.
- [ ] Essentials in carry on.
