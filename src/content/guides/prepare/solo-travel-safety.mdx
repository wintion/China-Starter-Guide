## Quick Answer
Solo travel is manageable with a simple safety routine: stay connected, use official transport, and keep your plan flexible.

## Why this matters
When you travel alone, you are your own backup. Small habits reduce risk and boost confidence.

## Step-by-step
1. Share your daily plan and lodging details with someone you trust.
2. Keep a power bank and a local SIM or data plan for reliable connectivity.
3. Use official ride hailing or taxi stands, especially at night.
4. Avoid isolated areas after dark and choose well lit routes.
5. Verify locally for any area specific guidance or restrictions.

## Common problems & fixes
- Phone battery dies: stop at a cafe to charge or use a power bank.
- You feel followed: enter a busy store and call for help.
- Language barrier at a checkpoint: show your passport and hotel address calmly.

## Checklist
- [ ] Daily plan shared with a contact.
- [ ] Power bank charged.
- [ ] Official transport apps installed.
- [ ] Offline copy of hotel address saved.
