## Quick Answer
Bargaining is common in markets but not in fixed price stores. Keep it friendly and walk away if needed.

## Why this matters
Knowing when to bargain prevents awkward moments and helps you avoid overpaying.

## Step-by-step
1. Ask if the price is fixed before negotiating.
2. Start with a polite counteroffer and a smile.
3. Keep the conversation short and respectful.
4. If the price does not work, thank the seller and move on.
5. Verify locally if a market has fixed price rules.

## Common problems & fixes
- Seller looks offended: apologize and pay the asked price or leave.
- Price is unclear: ask for the total in writing.
- You feel pressured: step away and return later if interested.

## Checklist
- [ ] Confirmed the price is negotiable.
- [ ] Friendly tone maintained.
- [ ] Maximum price set in advance.
- [ ] Willingness to walk away.
