## Quick Answer
Keep compliments simple and sincere. Use short phrases like "Thank you" and "This is very good".

## Why this matters
Clear politeness builds trust and helps you get help when needed.

## Step-by-step
1. Use short compliments focused on service or a product.
2. Say "Thank you" after help and when receiving items.
3. If you need assistance, start with "Excuse me".
4. Keep your tone calm and avoid overly personal comments.
5. Verify locally for formal settings such as hotels or clinics.

## Common problems & fixes
- Compliment misunderstood: switch to a simple thanks and move on.
- You feel awkward: keep it brief and polite.
- Communication breaks down: use a translation app and a short note.

## Checklist
- [ ] Basic polite phrases ready.
- [ ] Calm tone maintained.
- [ ] Short, neutral compliments used.
- [ ] Translation backup available.
