## Quick Answer
Be polite, avoid pushing sensitive topics, and follow local cues in public spaces.

## Why this matters
Respectful behavior helps you get better service and smoother interactions.

## Step-by-step
1. Observe how people line up, speak, and move in shared spaces.
2. Keep your voice moderate in restaurants, trains, and shops.
3. Use short, clear requests such as "Excuse me" and "Thank you".
4. Avoid intense debates in casual settings.
5. Verify locally for venue specific etiquette.

## Common problems & fixes
- You are unsure how to act: follow local cues and keep it simple.
- A conversation feels tense: switch to a neutral topic or exit politely.
- You feel rushed in a line: stay calm and wait your turn.

## Checklist
- [ ] Calm tone ready.
- [ ] Neutral topics prepared.
- [ ] Awareness of local cues.
- [ ] Patience in crowded areas.
