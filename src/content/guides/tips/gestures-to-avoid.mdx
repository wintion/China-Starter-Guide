## Quick Answer
Avoid pointing at people, touching strangers, or making big gestures in close spaces. Use open hand gestures instead.

## Why this matters
Body language can be misread. Simple, respectful gestures reduce confusion.

## Step-by-step
1. Use an open hand to indicate directions instead of pointing at people.
2. Keep personal space and avoid touching strangers.
3. In crowded spaces, keep gestures small and close to your body.
4. If you need attention, use a polite verbal cue like "Excuse me".
5. Verify locally for venue specific expectations.

## Common problems & fixes
- You gesture too broadly in a crowd: step aside and speak calmly.
- Someone looks uncomfortable: apologize and adjust your distance.
- You cannot be heard: move closer and speak clearly.

## Checklist
- [ ] Open hand gestures used.
- [ ] Personal space respected.
- [ ] Calm verbal cue ready.
- [ ] Body language kept neutral.
