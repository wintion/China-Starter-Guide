## Quick Answer
Choose routes with fewer stairs and use taxis or Didi for door to door travel when needed.

## Why this matters
Large stations and long walks can be tiring. A simple accessibility plan improves comfort and safety.

## Step-by-step
1. Select stations with elevator access and shorter transfer paths.
2. Use taxis or Didi for the last mile when walking is difficult.
3. Request help from station staff if you need assistance.
4. Keep medical information and emergency contacts accessible.
5. Verify locally for accessibility features and station layouts.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: The elevator is out of service.
  Resolution: Use a taxi or move to a station with working elevators.
- Scenario: Long walk between platforms.
  Resolution: Ask staff for the shortest accessible route or take a taxi instead.

## Common problems & fixes
- Crowded platforms: wait for a less crowded car or travel off peak.
- Limited seating: ask staff for priority seating guidance.
- Confusing signage: use a map app and ask staff for help.

## Checklist
- [ ] Accessible stations identified.
- [ ] Taxi or Didi option ready.
- [ ] Medical info carried.
- [ ] Extra time added for transfers.
