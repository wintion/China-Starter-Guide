## Quick Answer
Stop in a safe spot, check your map, and return to a known landmark. Use translation tools or ask staff for help.

## Why this matters
Feeling lost is stressful. A simple plan keeps you safe and helps you find your route faster.

## Step-by-step
1. Move to a safe, well lit area or inside a nearby shop.
2. Open your map app and check your last known landmark.
3. Compare street names or station exits with your notes.
4. Ask staff or police for directions using a short translated phrase.
5. Verify locally if the route differs from the map.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: You exit the metro on the wrong side of a large road.
  Resolution: Re enter the station and follow exit signs to the correct side.
- Scenario: Your phone battery is low.
  Resolution: Pause in a cafe to charge or use a taxi with a clear destination note.

## Common problems & fixes
- Map shows the wrong location: wait for GPS to stabilize and try again.
- You cannot communicate: show the map pin and Chinese address.
- You feel unsafe: return to a busy area and use a taxi or Didi.

## Checklist
- [ ] Safe location chosen.
- [ ] Map and address checked.
- [ ] Help requested with translation.
- [ ] Backup transport ready.
