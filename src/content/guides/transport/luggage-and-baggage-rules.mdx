## Quick Answer
Check size and weight rules for your transport, keep essentials in a carry on, and label your bags.

## Why this matters
Baggage rules vary by airline and train. A simple packing plan prevents delays and fees.

## Step-by-step
1. Check your airline or train baggage rules and verify locally for updates.
2. Pack valuables, documents, and a change of clothes in your carry on.
3. Label your bag with a name and phone number.
4. Use overhead racks or designated luggage areas on trains.
5. Keep your bag within sight during stops.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: Your bag is too large for the rack.
  Resolution: Use station luggage storage or switch to a taxi for the final leg.
- Scenario: Your bag goes missing at arrival.
  Resolution: Report it at the service desk before leaving the station or airport.

## Common problems & fixes
- Overweight baggage: move items to your carry on or pay the fee.
- No storage space: ask staff for the correct luggage area.
- Confusing restrictions: check posted rules at the station.

## Checklist
- [ ] Rules checked for each leg.
- [ ] Essentials in carry on.
- [ ] Bags labeled clearly.
- [ ] Storage plan ready.
