## Quick Answer
Metro is usually the fastest choice for busy corridors and city centers. Use it when you have light luggage and the line is running.

## Why this matters
Metro systems can be fast and affordable, but station layouts and transfers can be confusing for first time visitors.

## Step-by-step
1. Check your route in a map app and note the line color and final station name.
2. Open a transit QR in your wallet app or buy a single ride ticket at the machine.
3. Scan at the gate and follow platform signs for your direction of travel.
4. Transfer only when needed and follow exit numbers to match your destination.
5. Verify last train times locally if you are traveling late.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: Your QR code does not load at the gate.
  Resolution: Switch to mobile data, reopen the wallet app, or buy a single ride ticket.
- Scenario: You are carrying heavy luggage during rush hour.
  Resolution: Take a taxi or Didi instead of squeezing into a crowded train.

## Common problems & fixes
- Wrong direction: step off at the next station and switch platforms.
- Station names hard to read: use the map app and compare characters with signage.
- Gate rejects your ticket: ask staff to reset the gate or issue a new ticket.

## Checklist
- [ ] Route checked and line color noted.
- [ ] Transit QR or ticket ready before entering.
- [ ] Exit number matched to destination.
- [ ] Backup plan for late night travel.
