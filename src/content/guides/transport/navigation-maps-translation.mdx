## Quick Answer
Save your destination in Chinese, use offline maps, and keep a translation tool ready for quick questions.

## Why this matters
Many signs have English, but drivers and staff may prefer Chinese addresses. A prepared map and translation workflow avoids confusion.

## Step-by-step
1. Save your hotel and key destinations in a map app and pin the entrances.
2. Download offline maps and enable GPS before leaving Wi-Fi.
3. Save the Chinese address and a nearby landmark in notes.
4. Use translation tools to show short phrases or camera translation.
5. Verify locally if place names or entrances differ from the map.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: A driver cannot read your English address.
  Resolution: Show the Chinese address and the pinned map location.
- Scenario: The map sends you to a back entrance.
  Resolution: Walk to the main entrance and update the pin for future rides.

## Common problems & fixes
- No data connection: use offline maps and ask staff for directions.
- Wrong station exit: compare exit numbers with your destination pin.
- App crashes: keep a paper note with the address.

## Checklist
- [ ] Destinations saved in map app.
- [ ] Chinese address copied to notes.
- [ ] Offline map downloaded.
- [ ] Translation tool ready.
