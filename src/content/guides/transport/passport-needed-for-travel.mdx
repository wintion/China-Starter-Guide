## Quick Answer
You usually need your original passport for trains, flights, and some ticket checks. Keep it accessible but secure.

## Why this matters
Missing ID can stop you from boarding. A simple plan for storage and access avoids last minute issues.

## Step-by-step
1. Pack your passport in a secure, easy to reach pocket on travel days.
2. Keep a paper copy and a secure photo as backups.
3. Show your passport at ticket counters and security checks as requested.
4. Store it in your hotel safe when not needed and verify locally for exceptions.
5. If you change plans, confirm you still have your passport before leaving.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: You left your passport at the hotel.
  Resolution: Return to the hotel before the trip or rebook if time is too short.
- Scenario: Your passport is lost.
  Resolution: Contact local authorities and your embassy, then verify locally for replacement steps.

## Common problems & fixes
- ID rejected: confirm your passport is valid and not damaged.
- Security asks for printed tickets: show the booking confirmation and ask for guidance.
- Staff cannot find your booking: show the booking number and passport.

## Checklist
- [ ] Passport packed in a secure place.
- [ ] Backup copy stored separately.
- [ ] Booking confirmation saved offline.
- [ ] Hotel safe used when not traveling.
