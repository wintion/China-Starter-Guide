## Quick Answer

As a foreign traveler in China, **your passport is your primary ID**.  
You need it for **long-distance travel, hotels, and official checks**, but **not for every daily activity**.  
Carry it when moving between cities; you can leave it safely at the hotel for short local outings.

---

## The Core Reality (Important to Understand)

China does **not** issue local ID cards to visitors.  
Your passport functions as:
- Identification
- Ticket verification
- Registration document

There is no alternative document that replaces it.

---

## Situations Where You MUST Have Your Passport

You need your passport for:

### 1) High-Speed Rail & Long-Distance Trains
- Ticket verification is tied to passport details
- You may need to show it at station gates or counters
- Boarding without it is not possible

Always carry it on travel days.

---

### 2) Domestic Flights
- Required for check-in
- Required at security
- Required at boarding

This is non-negotiable.

---

### 3) Hotels & Accommodation
- Mandatory for check-in
- Used for police registration
- Copies or photos are **not sufficient**

Hotels cannot legally skip this step.

---

### 4) Police or Official Checks (Rare but Possible)
- Passport may be requested
- Usually brief and routine
- Cooperation resolves it quickly

This is uncommon but legitimate.

---

## Situations Where You Usually Do NOT Need Your Passport

You typically do **not** need to carry your passport for:
- Short walks
- Dining
- Shopping
- Using metro or buses
- Ride-hailing
- Tourist attractions (some exceptions apply)

A phone and payment apps are usually enough.

---

## How Locals and Long-Stay Foreigners Handle This

Common practice:
- Carry passport on travel days
- Leave it locked in hotel safe otherwise
- Carry a **photo or scan** as backup (not official, but helpful)

This balances safety and convenience.

---

## What NOT to Do

- Do not rely only on a passport photo for travel
- Do not leave passport in checked luggage
- Do not forget it on train or flight days
- Do not argue if asked to present it

Treat it as essential travel gear.

---

## Common First-Time Mistakes

- Carrying passport everywhere unnecessarily
- Forgetting it on travel days
- Assuming hotel copies are enough
- Storing it loosely in bags

Simple habits prevent issues.

---

## Safety Tips for Carrying Your Passport

- Use a secure inner pocket or pouch
- Avoid taking it out unnecessarily
- Return it to safe storage after use

Loss is rare but inconvenient.

---

## Reality Check

Most travelers find:
> “I only needed my passport on travel days and at hotels.”

This is the normal pattern.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Plan travel days carefully
- Carry ID only when required
- Store documents securely

You can follow this routine confidently.

---

- [ ] Passport available for hotel check-in.
- [ ] Safe storage arranged at hotel.
- [ ] Backup photo stored on phone.
- [ ] Travel schedule checked.
