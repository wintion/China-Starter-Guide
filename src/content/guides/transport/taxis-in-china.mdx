## Quick Answer
Use official taxi queues or clearly marked cabs. Confirm the meter is on and pay by QR code or cash.

## Why this matters
Taxis are useful for short trips or areas without metro access, but unofficial drivers can overcharge or refuse meters.

## Step-by-step
1. Look for official taxi stands at airports, stations, or busy streets.
2. Show your destination in Chinese and confirm the driver starts the meter.
3. Keep the ride short and direct by showing the map if needed.
4. Pay by scanning the taxi QR code or showing your pay code.
5. Keep the receipt or take a photo of the taxi number if needed.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: A driver refuses to use the meter.
  Resolution: Exit and take another official taxi or use Didi.
- Scenario: The driver asks for cash only.
  Resolution: Pay cash if you are comfortable, or choose another taxi and use QR payment.

## Common problems & fixes
- Taxi hard to find in rain: go to a main road or use a ride hailing app.
- Driver cannot read English: show the destination in Chinese or point on the map.
- Payment rejected: use cash or another wallet and verify locally.

## Checklist
- [ ] Official taxi stand used when possible.
- [ ] Meter started at the beginning.
- [ ] Payment method ready before arrival.
- [ ] Taxi number recorded for issues.
