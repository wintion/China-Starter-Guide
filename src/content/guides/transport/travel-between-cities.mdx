## Quick Answer
Choose high speed rail for city center to city center trips, and choose flights for longer distances or limited train seats. Verify locally for timetables.

## Why this matters
Intercity travel shapes your itinerary. Picking the right mode saves hours and reduces fatigue.

## Step-by-step
1. Compare distance, travel time, and total door to door time.
2. If stations are central and trains are frequent, rail is often easiest.
3. If the route is long or mountainous, flights may be more efficient.
4. Consider luggage, budget, and transfers when deciding.
5. Book early for peak periods and verify locally for schedule changes.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: You have a tight morning schedule and heavy luggage.
  Resolution: Choose a direct taxi or Didi to the station and a train with minimal transfers.
- Scenario: Your preferred train is sold out.
  Resolution: Look for flights or adjust your city order to fit available seats.

## Common problems & fixes
- Wrong station booked: rebook or plan extra time to reach the correct station.
- Overpacked itinerary: drop a day trip and add rest time.
- Last minute changes: keep a flexible budget and buffer days.

## Checklist
- [ ] Door to door time compared.
- [ ] Tickets booked with correct stations.
- [ ] Luggage plan confirmed.
- [ ] Buffer time added for changes.
