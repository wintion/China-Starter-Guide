## Quick Answer
Plan extra time, avoid tight transfers, and choose door to door transport when your schedule is tight.

## Why this matters
Children change the pace of travel. A flexible plan keeps the day calm and reduces stress for everyone.

## Step-by-step
1. Choose transport with fewer transfers and short walks.
2. Use Didi or taxis for door to door travel when schedules are tight.
3. Identify family friendly entrances and elevators in stations.
4. Pack snacks, water, and a small comfort kit for delays.
5. Verify locally for stroller access and family facilities.

### Traveler scenarios
- Scenario: Your child is tired before a long transfer.
  Resolution: Pause, use a taxi for the next leg, and reduce walking.
- Scenario: A stroller does not fit through a gate.
  Resolution: Use a wide gate or ask staff for assistance.

## Common problems & fixes
- Missed connection: rebook the next train or adjust the day plan.
- Crowded platforms: wait behind the safety line and board last.
- No family room available: ask staff for a quiet waiting area.

## Checklist
- [ ] Extra time built into the day.
- [ ] Snacks and water packed.
- [ ] Elevator access confirmed.
- [ ] Backup transport plan ready.
