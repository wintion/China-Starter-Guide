## Quick Answer

In China, **almost everyone uses ride-hailing apps**.  
Street taxis are increasingly hard to flag down—especially during rush hours or at night.  
If you install **Didi**, you can move around Chinese cities easily without speaking Chinese.

---

## The Core Reality (This Is Critical)

China is **not** a street-taxi culture anymore.

Locals:
- Rarely hail taxis by hand
- Rely almost entirely on apps
- Expect rides to be tracked, priced, and paid digitally

Trying to flag taxis manually is often **slow and frustrating**.

---

## What Is Didi?

Didi is:
- China’s main ride-hailing platform
- Used nationwide
- Integrated with digital payments
- Designed for daily commuting, not tourism

It is the **default** way people take taxis.

---

## Why Didi Is Essential for Visitors

Didi solves multiple problems at once:
- No language negotiation
- No cash handling
- No route arguments
- No price guessing
- Driver details are recorded

This dramatically reduces friction.

---

## How Booking a Ride Works (Simple Flow)

1. Open the app.
2. Set pickup location (GPS or pin).
3. Set destination (map or address).
4. Confirm car type.
5. Wait for driver assignment.
6. Get picked up.
7. Ride and pay automatically.

No conversation required.

---

## Pickup Points: What Foreigners Miss

Drivers expect:
- You to be **at the pickup pin**
- Not vague street descriptions
- Quick boarding

If the pickup is unclear:
- Walk to the pinned location
- Use building entrances
- Avoid stopping traffic

Precise pickup saves time.

---

## Payment (Very Important)

- Payment is **cashless**
- Charged automatically
- Uses Alipay or WeChat Pay

You do **not** pay the driver directly.

---

## Language Reality

- Drivers usually do **not** speak English
- This is normal
- The app handles everything

If needed:
- Use in-app chat
- Send preset messages
- Share location

Avoid phone calls if possible.

---

## When Didi Might Be Slow

Expect delays during:
- Morning and evening rush hours
- Heavy rain
- Major holidays
- Late-night peak hours

This affects locals too.

---

## What NOT to Do

- Do not hail aggressively on the street
- Do not negotiate price
- Do not pay cash unless explicitly required
- Do not cancel repeatedly without reason

The app handles rules.

---

## Common First-Time Mistakes

- Expecting street taxis to stop
- Standing far from pickup pin
- Panicking when driver doesn’t call
- Trying to explain directions verbally

Let the app do the work.

---

## Reality Check

Many visitors say:
> “Once I used Didi, everything became much easier.”

This is extremely common.

---

## What Locals Do Instead

- Use Didi for most trips
- Avoid street hailing
- Trust GPS routing
- Pay automatically

You are using the same system.
